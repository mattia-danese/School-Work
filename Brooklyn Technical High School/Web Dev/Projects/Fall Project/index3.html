This is a pushup
To do a pushup, make sure your hands are under your shoulders and that your back is straight. For the full range of motion, be sure to go down untill your elbow is at a 90 degree angle.
This is a squat
To do a squat, make sure your feet are shoulder width apart, your back is straight, and to push through your heels.
This is a planche
To do a planche, make sure your back is kept parallel to the floor and your abs are flexed for the entire duration.
This is a inverted row
To do an inverted row, go under a bar and pull yourself up so your chest is inline with hands. If it gets too easy, move your feet farther out or elevate them off the ground.
This is a dip
To do a dip, support yourself on handle bars and lower your body so your elbow makes a 90 degree angle. Then, push yourself back up.
This is a wallsit
To do a wallsit, imagine you're sitting on an invsible chair. Set your back against a wall, make sure your knees create a 90 degree angle, and hold.
This is a scissor kick
To do scissor kicks, lay on your back and place your arms by your sides. Raise your leg as high as you can and bring it back down. Alternate. This shoud be quick movement.
This is a pullup
To do a pullup, place your hands shoudler width apart on the pullup bar. Pull yourself up so your chin gets above the bar. Focus on bringing your shoulder blades together.
This is a muscle up
To do a muscle up, start by doing a pullup, then switch your grip to that of a dip, and finish by pushing yourself up and doing a dip.
This is a pistol squat
To do a pistol squat, balance on one leg and put the other out in front of you. Go down slowly. Your unbalancing leg should straighten as you go down.
This is a front lever
To do a front lever, hang on a pullup bar and bring your legs up, together, and lean back. Your body should be parallel with the floor. Flex your abs and hold.
This is the flag pole
To do a flag pole, get a good grip on a sturdy poll, hands should be a little farther out than pullup grip. Lift your legs and maintain balance with arms. Tighten core so legs don't fall down.
